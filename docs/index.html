How It Works

Three steps, then you're done

01 — MAP
Create your snippets in the popup
Click the SnipKit icon. Hit "+ New snippet." Map a trigger like \stack to any block of text. Your tech stack, your bio, your PR template — anything you retype.
02 — TYPE
Use your trigger in any text field
Go to any webpage. Click into any input, textarea, or editor. Type your trigger. SnipKit watches as you type — no hotkey, no menu, no interruption to your flow.
03 — EXPAND
Watch it expand instantly
The trigger is replaced by your full text the moment you finish typing it. Cursor lands right at the end. Keep going — the form fills itself.
Features

Built for real developer workflows

⚡
Works everywhere
Standard inputs, textareas, contenteditable editors (GitHub, Notion, Linear). For canvas-based editors like Google Docs, the command palette copies to clipboard in one keypress.
⌨
Command palette
Press Ctrl+Shift+Space on any page to open a floating search palette. Arrow keys to navigate, Enter to select. Works even on Google Docs.
📂
Organized library
Tag snippets as Work, Hackathon, GitHub, or Other. Filter by category. Search by trigger or expansion text. Usage counter shows which snippets pull their weight.
📦
Import & Export
Your snippet library exports as clean JSON. Import from any compatible file. Back up, migrate between Chrome profiles, or share your library with your team.
🔒
Your data never leaves your browser
SnipKit has no backend, no account, no analytics, and no network requests. Snippets are stored in chrome.storage.local — on your device, under your control. Read the full privacy policy →
